Biryani

    ingredients

  • 2 cups Basmati rice 500 grams chicken (cut into pieces) 2 large onions (sliced) 2 tomatoes (chopped) 1/2 cup yogurt 4 cloves garlic (minced) 1-inch ginger (grated) 2 green chilies (slit) 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves 1 teaspoon red chili powder 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1 teaspoon biryani masala powder 4 cups water Salt to taste 4 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) Whole spices: 1 bay leaf, 4-5 cloves, 4-5 peppercorns, 1-inch cinnamon stick, 2-3 green cardamom pods

    • procedure

    • Here's the procedure for making Biryani:
    • 1. Wash and soak Basmati rice for 30 minutes.
    • 2. Heat ghee in a pot, add whole spices and sauté.
    • 3. Add sliced onions, cook until golden brown.
    • 4. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies, sauté until fragrant.
    • 5. Add chopped tomatoes, cook until soft.
    • 6. Add chicken pieces, cook until white on the outside.
    • 7. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, and biryani masala, mix well.
    • 8. Add yogurt, mint leaves, and coriander leaves, stir to combine.
    • 9. Drain soaked rice and add to the pot, mix gently.
    • 10. Add water and salt, bring to a boil.
    • 11. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes until rice is cooked and chicken is tender.
    • 12. Let it rest covered for 5-10 minutes.
    • 13. Fluff with a fork before serving. Serve hot with raita or salad.

Upma

  • Semolina (Rava) - 1 cup Water - 2 cups Oil or Ghee - 2-3 tablespoons Mustard Seeds - 1 teaspoon Urad Dal (Split Black Gram) - 1 teaspoon Chana Dal (Split Chickpeas) - 1 teaspoon Curry Leaves - 8-10 leaves Green Chilies - 2, chopped Ginger - 1-inch piece, finely chopped Onion - 1 medium-sized, finely chopped Carrot - 1 small, finely chopped Green Peas - 1/4 cup (optional) Salt - to taste Lemon Juice - 1 tablespoon (optional) Coriander Leaves - 2 tablespoons, chopped (for garnishing)

      • Procedure

    • Here's the procedure for making Upma:
      1. Heat 2-3 tablespoons of oil or ghee in a pan.
      2. Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and let them splutter.
      3. Add 1 teaspoon of urad dal (split black gram) and 1 teaspoon of chana dal (split chickpeas). Sauté until they turn golden brown.
      4. Add 8-10 curry leaves, 2 chopped green chilies, and 1-inch piece of finely chopped ginger. Sauté for a minute.
      5. Add 1 medium-sized finely chopped onion. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
      6. Add 1 small finely chopped carrot and 1/4 cup of green peas (optional). Sauté for a couple of minutes.
      7. Now, add 1 cup of semolina (rava) to the pan. Roast the semolina on low flame until it turns aromatic and slightly golden brown. This step is crucial to prevent lumps in the Upma.
      8. Meanwhile, boil 2 cups of water separately.
      9. Once the semolina is roasted, slowly add the boiling water to it while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
      10. Season with salt to taste and mix well.
      11. Cover the pan and let the Upma cook on low flame for about 3-4 minutes or until the semolina absorbs all the water and becomes fluffy.
      12. Turn off the heat and let the Upma sit covered for a couple of minutes.
      13. Finally, squeeze in 1 tablespoon of lemon juice (optional) and garnish with 2 tablespoons of chopped coriander leaves.
      14. Give it a gentle mix and serve hot.
